**Goal**: Map how they learn new work skills, absorb feedback, and take in professional information. Present a/b/c/d options with each question.

## Questions

**1.1 Professional Learning Style**
"When you need to learn a new tool, skill, or system for work, what do you do?

a) Read the documentation or a how-to guide
b) Watch a video tutorial or walkthrough
c) Ask a colleague or friend to show me
d) Just jump in and figure it out by trial and error

...or tell me in your own words."

**1.2 Meeting Behaviour**
"When you're in a meeting or group discussion about work, what do you do?

a) Take detailed notes - I write everything down
b) Listen carefully and process internally - no notes needed
c) Ask questions live - I learn by engaging
d) Honestly, I zone out and catch up later

...or tell me in your own words."

**1.3 Big Picture vs. How-To**
"When someone gives you a new task, what do you need first?

a) The big picture - WHY are we doing this? What's the goal?
b) The how-to - just tell me the steps so I can start
c) Both - give me the why AND the steps
d) An example of what the finished product looks like

...or tell me in your own words."

**1.4 Feedback Absorption**
"When a manager, lecturer, or supervisor gives you critical feedback, what happens?

a) I process it on the spot and start adjusting immediately
b) I need time - I take it away and think about it before I can act on it
c) I get emotional first (frustrated, deflated) then rational kicks in later
d) I want specifics - 'this is wrong' isn't helpful, show me what to fix

...or tell me in your own words."

**1.5 Industry Learning**
"How do you keep up with what's happening in your field or industry?

a) I actively read articles, newsletters, or industry content
b) I learn by doing - I pick things up on the job
c) Through conversations - I learn from people, not pages
d) I don't really keep up - I focus on what's in front of me

...or tell me in your own words."

**1.6 Skill Acquisition Speed**
"When you need to pick up a completely new skill, how quickly can you get going?

a) Fast - I'm a self-starter, give me a laptop and wifi
b) Medium - I need some structured training or a course first
c) Depends on the complexity - simple stuff fast, complex stuff needs time
d) Slow - I like to feel confident before I start doing

...or tell me in your own words."

**1.7 Information Filtering**
"You've just been given a long project brief or dense document. What do you do?

a) Read the whole thing cover to cover
b) Skim for the key points, headings, and action items
c) Ask someone to give me the summary
d) Read the intro and conclusion, skip the middle

...or tell me in your own words."
