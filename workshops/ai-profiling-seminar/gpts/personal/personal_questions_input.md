**Goal**: Map their complete learning profile. Present a/b/c/d options with each question, then add "...or tell me in your own words."

## Questions

**1.1 Format Preference**
"When you need to learn something new for uni - a concept you don't understand yet - what do you actually do?

a) Watch a YouTube video or tutorial
b) Read the textbook or an article
c) Ask a friend or classmate to explain it
d) Google until I find a summary that clicks

...or tell me in your own words."

**1.2 Depth vs. Breadth**
"When you find a topic interesting, how deep do you go?

a) I go deep - reading 5 articles, watching documentaries, falling down rabbit holes
b) I get the key idea and move on to the next thing
c) Somewhere in between - depends on the topic
d) I go deep only if it's relevant to an assignment or exam

...or tell me in your own words."

**1.3 Structure Preference**
"Do you learn better with clear structure or by exploring?

a) Give me Step 1, Step 2, Step 3 - I like a clear path
b) Let me tinker and figure it out myself
c) A rough framework, then I explore within it
d) Depends on the subject - some need structure, others don't

...or tell me in your own words."

**1.4 Retention Method**
"After a lecture or study session, what actually helps the content STICK? Be honest - what you ACTUALLY do, not what you think you should do.

a) Rewrite or reorganise my notes
b) Talk about it with friends or explain it to someone
c) Do practice problems or apply it
d) Honestly, I just hope it sticks - maybe re-read before the exam

...or tell me in your own words."

**1.5 Environment & Conditions**
"Where do you do your best learning?

a) Total silence - library, quiet room
b) Background noise - cafe, lo-fi music
c) With other people around - study group energy
d) Late at night in my room when everyone's asleep

...or tell me in your own words."

**1.6 Motivation Pattern**
"What makes you WANT to learn something?

a) Genuine curiosity about the topic
b) The pressure of a deadline or exam
c) Competing with classmates or wanting top marks
d) Fear of failing or falling behind

...or tell me in your own words."

**1.7 Information Overwhelm**
"When you're given too much information - a massive reading list, a dense 2-hour lecture - what's your instinct?

a) Power through everything start to finish
b) Skim for the key points and ignore the rest
c) Break it into chunks across days
d) Avoid it until the pressure forces me to deal with it

...or tell me in your own words."
