## Section Details

### 1. The Hook  -  AI Is Coming For Your Skills (5 min)

**Goal**: Create urgency. Make it personal.

- Open with a live ChatGPT demo: ask it to "write a professional email" â†’ show the generic output
- Ask the room: *"How many of you use ChatGPT the same way  -  open a new chat, ask a question, get a generic answer?"*
- Drop the line: *"In the AI era, your **profile** is your **strategy**. If AI doesn't know who you are, it treats you like everyone else. And 'everyone else' is replaceable."*
- Show the poster / slide: **Day 1 vs Day 30**  -  generic AI vs personalised AI

### 3. The Demo  -  Same Question, Two AIs (10 min)

**Goal**: Show  -  don't tell  -  why profiling matters. This is the WOW moment.

**Setup**: Two ChatGPT Projects open side by side (or switch tabs).
- **Project A**: Empty instructions (generic ChatGPT)
- **Project B**: Loaded with a sample student's PIC profile

**Run 3 comparative prompts, revealing the difference:**

**Demo 1  -  Learning (Input difference)**
> Prompt: *"Explain the concept of cognitive load theory to me"*
- **Generic AI**: Standard textbook explanation, paragraphs, formal
- **Profiled AI**: Knows this student prefers video-style summaries and learns big-picture first â†’ gives a metaphor, then 3 bullet points, then offers to go deeper

**Demo 2  -  Problem-solving (Process difference)**
> Prompt: *"I'm stuck on my essay. I don't know how to structure the argument."*
- **Generic AI**: Gives a template (intro, body, conclusion)
- **Profiled AI**: Knows this student is intuitive and processes externally â†’ asks guiding questions instead of giving answers, offers to brainstorm out loud

**Demo 3  -  Output stretching (Compass in action)**
> Prompt: *"Help me write a summary of my research findings"*
- **Generic AI**: Writes a bullet-point summary
- **Profiled AI**: Writes in their preferred format (bullets) BUT adds: *"I noticed you always default to bullet points. Want me to draft this as a short narrative paragraph too? Building narrative writing is in your growth areas."*

**Pause and ask the room**: *"Which AI would you rather use every day  -  the one that treats you like a stranger, or the one that knows your brain?"*

### 4. The Tool  -  Meet Your Profile GPTs (5 min)

**Goal**: Explain the system and the workflow.

- Show the 3 GPTs: **Personal** | **Social** | **Professional**
- Each one is a 15-20 min conversation that profiles you across Input, Process, Output
- At the end, you get a **PIC instruction set**:
  - **Layer 1: Persona**  -  who you are
  - **Layer 2: Interface**  -  how AI should serve you
  - **Layer 3: Compass**  -  how AI should grow you (70% serve, 30% stretch)
- You paste the instructions into a **ChatGPT Project**
- **Memory** handles evolution  -  your AI learns and adapts over time
- When you've changed, come back to the Profile GPT and rebuild â†’ paste the new version

**Show the workflow visually:**
```
Profile GPT â†’ 15-min chat â†’ PIC instruction set â†’ paste into Project â†’ Memory evolves â†’ revisit to upgrade
```

### 5. Hands-On  -  Build Your Personal Profile (20 min)

**Goal**: Students build their first profile RIGHT NOW.

- Share the link to **Student GPT - Personal**
- Students open it on their phones or laptops
- The GPT walks them through ~15 questions conversationally
- At the end, they get their PIC Personal instruction set

**Facilitator tips:**
- Walk the room. Help anyone stuck.
- After 15 min, announce: *"If you're not quite done, that's fine  -  you can finish after. But let's look at what the GPT produced for those of you who are done."*
- Ask 2-3 volunteers to share one surprising observation from their profile

### 6. The Reveal  -  Load It, Test It, See the Difference (7 min)

**Goal**: Students see their OWN personalised AI for the first time.

- Guide students through:
  1. Create a new ChatGPT Project (e.g., "My Study Partner")
  2. Paste their instruction set into Project Instructions
  3. Start a conversation: *"Explain [topic from their course] to me"*
- They see the AI respond in THEIR style  -  their format, their tone, their depth

- Ask: *"Does that feel different from normal ChatGPT?"*
- Point out: *"That's just Day 1. Memory hasn't kicked in yet. Day 30 is when it gets scary good."*
