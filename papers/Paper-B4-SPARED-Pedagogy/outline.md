# OUTLINE: Paper B4 — SPARED Pedagogy
# "SPARED: A Five-Phase Emergence Design for Teaching Dialectical Thinking"

**Venue:** *Journal of Management Education* / *Active Learning in Higher Education*
**Format:** Pedagogical Innovation Paper
**Word Target:** 5,000-6,000 words

---

## Section Map

### 1. Introduction (600 words)
- The AI-era critical thinking crisis: students can evaluate but not generate disagreement
- Preview of SPARED as a structured emergence model
- Contribution: a cognitive phase-transition pedagogy, not just a classroom activity

### 2. Literature Review (1,000 words)
- 2.1 Bloom's Taxonomy: levels without transition mechanics
- 2.2 Active learning frameworks: Think-Pair-Share, POGIL, PBL
- 2.3 Dialectical thinking: Basseches, King & Kitchener, Perry
- 2.4 Structured Academic Controversy (Johnson & Johnson)
- 2.5 The gap: no model designs for EMERGENCE as the learning outcome

### 3. The SPARED Model (1,500 words)
- 3.1 Overview: 5 phases as cognitive state transitions
- 3.2 SPARK (Earth): encounter the tension, ground in reality
- 3.3 POSITION (Water): take a stance, commit to a view
- 3.4 ARGUE (Fire): engage the opposite, structured friction
- 3.5 RECONCILE (Air): release attachment, find the synthesis
- 3.6 EMERGE (Space): the insight that could not be predicted from any single phase
- 3.7 The Pancha Bootham scaffold: why elemental metaphor aids cognitive progression

### 4. Instructional Design (1,000 words)
- 4.1 Classroom implementation protocol (90-minute session)
- 4.2 Worked example: "Should organizations prioritize efficiency or resilience?"
- 4.3 Assessment rubric: how to evaluate dialectical quality (not just argument quality)
- 4.4 Solo SPAR variant: the individual metacognitive practice

### 5. Discussion (600 words)
- Theoretical contribution: emergence as a pedagogical design target
- Practical contribution: a replicable protocol for dialectical skill development
- Limitations: no empirical classroom data yet (design paper, not evaluation paper)
- Future work: controlled classroom trials, learning outcome measurement

### 6. Conclusion (300 words)
- Dialectical thinking is learnable. SPARED provides the design.
