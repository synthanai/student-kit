## நோக்கம் / Intent (Soul: WHY)

### The Problem

1. **Critical thinking education teaches evaluation, not generative disagreement.** Students learn to assess arguments but not to construct productive counter-positions.
2. **Pedagogical models describe cognitive levels without transition mechanics.** Bloom's Taxonomy names 6 levels but doesn't specify how learners move from "Analyze" to "Evaluate" to "Create."
3. **AI-era learning demands dialectical fluency.** As AI automates factual recall and basic analysis, the human value proposition shifts to judgment, synthesis, and productive disagreement.
4. **No pedagogical model uses elemental cosmology as cognitive scaffolding.** The Pancha Bootham (5 elements: Earth, Water, Fire, Air, Space) provides a sensory-metaphorical scaffold that maps naturally to cognitive phases.

### The Purpose

A pedagogy paper that:
- **Introduces** SPARED as a 5-phase emergence design for dialectical thinking
- **Maps** each phase to a Pancha Bootham element and a cognitive state
- **Provides** classroom implementation guidance with worked examples
- **Positions** against Bloom's, Think-Pair-Share, Socratic Method, and POGIL
- **Targets** *Journal of Management Education* or *Active Learning in Higher Education*

### What We're Avoiding

| Anti-Pattern | Why | How We Prevent It |
|--------------|-----|-------------------|
| **Cultural appropriation** | Pancha Bootham is Tamil heritage | Frame as author's heritage (Tamil-first principle), not borrowed |
| **Mysticism** | Academic venues reject esoteric claims | Present elemental mapping as METAPHORICAL scaffold, not cosmological truth |
| **No evidence** | Pedagogy venues demand classroom data | Include instructional design + provide replicable protocol for classroom testing |

## வடிவம் / Abstraction (Mind: WHAT TYPE)

### Problem Type

**DESIGN**: Design a pedagogical model for teaching dialectical thinking through structured emergence.

### The 5 Phases

| Phase | Element | Tamil | Cognitive State | Activity |
|-------|---------|-------|-----------------|----------|
| SPARK | Earth (நிலம்) | Grounding | Encounter the problem | Present the tension |
| POSITION | Water (நீர்) | Flow | Take a stance | Commit to a perspective |
| ARGUE | Fire (நெருப்பு) | Friction | Engage the opposite | SPAR-style dialectic |
| RECONCILE | Air (காற்று) | Release | Synthesize tensions | Find the third way |
| EMERGE | Space (ஆகாயம்) | Opening | Create new understanding | The insight that couldn't be predicted |
